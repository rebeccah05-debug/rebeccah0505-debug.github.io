    Let the Sims Begin

    Welcome to The Sims 4! The Sims 4 is a life simulation game where players create characters (sims) and control their day-to-day lives. Gameplay is open-ended with a variety of ways to play, including designing homes, fostering relationships, and choosing careers. There's no wrong way to play -- just have fun!

    About this Manual

    This manual is meant to help new players get started and gain confidence by overviewing general applications of the game.

    This guide overviews:

    • Create-A-Sim (CAS): Design sims and customize their traits, skills, aspirations, and appearance.
    • Build mode (BM): Build and decorate your sim‚Äôs home.
    • Live mode (LM): Manage needs, emotions, and lives of your sims.

    This guide centers on PC controls. Here is a guide for Playstation and Xbox controls.

    Create-a-Sim

    Whether you‚Äôre making yourself, your neighbor, or your celebrity crush, here's how you create a sim!

    When starting in CAS, the game prompts you to pick to Create a Story or Start from Scratch.

    Creating a story expedites the creation process by asking questions and assigning your sim traits, aspirations, and an appearance. If you‚Äôre unhappy with the outcome, everything is changeable. Saying, ‚ÄúNo Thanks‚Äù to the story will drop you into customization. You can also download other players‚Äô sims from the gallery.

    Traits and Aspirations

    Traits are cornerstones of your sim‚Äôs personality, determining how they interact with their environment.

    Traits are permanent once you finish your sim.

    Aspirations function as your sim‚Äôs ultimate life goal.

    Click the hexagons in the upper left of the screen to access traits and aspirations. Likes and dislikes, found beneath the panel, are best left unchosen, as your sim develops them in-game.

    Appearance

    Appearance refers to the physical and biological aspects of your sim.

    Click the diamond in the top left to alter a sim‚Äôs biology, including gender, age, voice, and relationship to other created sims.

    Clicking on your sim opens the appearance wheel. Clicking different body parts provides different choices for clothing and body modifications.

    Above the column listing clothing choices, there is a row of icons, each relating to a specific social setting. The type of clothing shown depends on the selected setting.

    • To the left of your sim, the two vertical sliders correspond to muscularity and weight.

    üí°TIP: If you want to precisely modify your sim, enter detail-edit-mode by clicking on the eyeglass icon to the left of your sim‚Äôs head.

    Finally, give your sim a name in the top left corner. Click the checkmark in the bottom right, and you‚Äôre done!

    Controls

    Before you begin playing as your sim, let‚Äôs overview the controls for BM and LM. Camera controls in these modes are identical and are for trackpad and mouse unless otherwise noted.

    Camera Controls

    • Moving the camera: Hold left click and drag, or use the WASD or arrow keys.
    • Readjusting camera angle: Hold right click and drag.
    • Zooming in/out: Use the scroll wheel on the mouse. For the trackpad, use two fingers in the middle and scroll up or down.
    • Tilting the camera: Hold down the scroll wheel on the mouse and move the whole mouse up and down. For the trackpad, hold down the command key and click the arrow keys.

    In-Game Controls

    • Controlling time: Use the panel in the bottom middle of the screen to speed up, slow down, or pause time.
    • Moving between floors of a building: Click the up and down arrows in the top right.
    • Switching wall views (i.e. full, cutaway, no walls): Click the room icon in the top right.

    So, you made your sim and are ready to release them into the world! But wait‚Äîthey need a house first!

    Wow, that‚Äôs a lot of stuff on the screen! No worries, we detail each feature below.

    The Grid

    There will be a white grid surrounding the floor of your lot. This is the buildable area, and the tiles that items will align to. It ensures the walls of your home and the items within don‚Äôt interrupt sims movements through the space.

    To hide the grid, press G.

    The Tool Bar

    The tool bar, at the top middle of the screen, holds these essential tools (from left to right):

    • Select
    • Eyedropper
    • Design
    • Sledgehammer
    • Undo and Redo
    • Save to Library
    • Move House
    • Bulldoze
      • Bulldoze Lot
      • Bulldoze Terrain
    • Time of day (only for simulating in BM)

    For an in-depth look at these tools and other aspects of BM, see this BM Guide by the SimsWiki.

    Lot Info Panel

    The Lot Info Panel, at the top left of the screen, contains essential information about your lot, including lot type, dimensions, value, and traits and challenges.

    Build Buy Menu

    The Build Buy Menu is the large panel at the bottom left of your screen with three categories. You can switch between categories by clicking on their respective icons flush to the left side of the menu.

    • Build Catalog (house icon): It‚Äôs the first tab opened when you enter BM, with a large interactive house image, and is the primary directory for items relevant to exterior decor and foundational building.
    • Objects-by-Room (chair-in-a-box icon): It brings up a large interactive image of a kitchen setting. To move between rooms, click the related icons located above the interactive image.
    • Objects-by-Function (single chair icon): It contains a drop down menu to select specific functions, like surface or plumbing.

    üí°TIP: You can resell any purchased item with the sledgehammer.

    There are many aspects to BM, so here are a few questions you might ask yourself as you‚Äôre exploring BM.

    ‚ÄúI want to push this object against the wall, but the game won‚Äôt let me. Why?‚Äù

    BM is one of the more complicated aspects of the game, but you‚Äôll learn more features as you progress. Right now, this overview is a great start to familiarize yourself with the basics of BM.

    To return to LM, click the two-people icon located in the top right menu.

    Live Mode

    You have your character and house, and now you‚Äôre ready to play as your sim! Here‚Äôs what to know about LM.

    Controlling Sims

    Look at your beautiful sim! Uh-Oh, where are they? If you can‚Äôt find them in the world, left-click their portrait in the household panel. To follow them, right click their household portrait.

    You can only control one sim at a time ‚Äî switch between sims by left clicking their household portraits.

    You aren‚Äôt just here to watch your sim ‚Äî you‚Äôre here to play as them!

    • Your sim's actions will queue in the order you choose them, and the icons appear in the bottom left corner.
    • Some actions continue until you tell your sim to stop by left clicking the action icon.

    • Actions with the orange circle are instant actions done by the player.

    • When your sim leaves a location without you following them, their household portrait grays out. Click the two-person icon in their portrait to see available actions.

    Needs

    What do we want? Needs! When do we want them? Now!

    The Needs tab is in the Utility Panel (UP) at the bottom right of the screen. Based on a sim‚Äôs needs, they will gain moodlets that determine their emotional state, displayed under their character portrait. Moodlets result from actions, events, and in-game experiences.

    • To satisfy a sim‚Äôs needs, queue actions related to the specific need.
      • Ex. Have them eat to satisfy their hunger.
    • If you fail to satisfy a sim‚Äôs needs, there may be consequences.
      • If they are too tired, they may faint!

    Wants and Aspirations

    You chose an aspiration for your sim when you made them, and to see the tasks to complete it, click the gold icon in the UP.

    üí°TIP: Completing the aspiration allows you to pick a new one for your sim and grants them a longer lifespan!

    Wants are the thought bubbles above your sim's character portrait. There are no consequences for not fulfilling these or aspirations.

    • Wants go away on their own unless you pin them by clicking the pushpin near them.
    • Completing a want or aspiration grants you aspiration points.
      • To spend these, go to the Aspiration tab and click ‚ÄúRewards Store.‚Äù
    • The Rewards Store has items and traits you may purchase for the selected sim.

    Skills

    Some wants and aspirations involve learning skills!

    • Your sim‚Äôs skills and their levels are under the Skills tab in the UP.

      • Don‚Äôt fret if this is blank! BM contains items that increase skills, or your sim can purchase a book on a skill.
        • To buy a book, click on any bookshelf.

    • Reaching higher levels of skills unlocks new abilities.

    • When your sim does a skill-increasing action, a bar appears above their head to show skill progress.
      • Most skill actions will only stop when you cancel the action.

    üí°TIP: The Handiness skill is obtained through fixing items. If your sim attempts to fix an electronic after already being electrocuted by it, they will die on the second attempt. Save your game before fixing electronics with a low Handiness skill!

    Some skills and their objects include Comedy (microphone), Gaming (computer), and Painting (easel). Check out this list of skills your sim can achieve.
